// About

What this assistant is

The Swarna Andhra GVA Assistant is a question-and-answer tool built by Pahlé India Foundation for the state capacity-building programme. It helps district, constituency, and mandal officers find clear answers about how state and district income is measured, and how to raise it.

Who it is for

  • District and constituency officers who need quick, reliable answers grounded in official material.
  • Master trainers preparing sessions on estimation methodology.
  • Planning teams looking at where a district has comparative advantage and what to do about it.

How it works

Every question runs a semantic search across the full corpus, the official DDP and GSVA source documents, the district data, and all 1,378 vision plans. Answers are generated from that material and always cite the source, so every claim can be traced back.

Where the answers come from

  • The official Guideline for Compilation of DDP Estimates from the National Statistics Office.
  • The Compilation of GSVA Estimates with base year 2022-23.
  • District GSDP, GDDP, and per-capita data for all 28 districts.
  • Vision and action plans for every constituency and mandal, plus sector case studies.
// Methodology

How district income is estimated

This follows the official Guideline for Compilation of District Domestic Product issued by the National Statistics Office, consistent with the revised GSVA series under base year 2022-23.

District Domestic Product (DDP) measures the total value of goods and services produced within a district over a year. It is the district-level counterpart of GSDP at the state level and GDP at the national level.

For estimation, the district economy is divided into eleven broad industries: agriculture and allied, mining, manufacturing, electricity and utilities, construction, trade and hotels, transport and communication, financial services, real estate and professional services, public administration, and other services.

The three approaches

Top-down

State to district

State totals are estimated first, then shared among districts using indicators. Used when direct district data is not available.

Bottom-up

District first

Values are estimated directly from data within each district, then added up to the state total. Reflects local conditions best.

Mixed

Best of both

Bottom-up where reliable district data exists, top-down for the rest. Balances accuracy against data availability.

From GVA to district income

GDVA = sum of adjusted GVA across all sectors
GDDP = GDVA + Taxes on Products − Subsidies on Products
NDDP = NDVA + Taxes − Subsidies, where NVA = GVA − Consumption of Fixed Capital

Per capita district income is the total district income divided by the mid-year projected population, measured in rupees per person.
