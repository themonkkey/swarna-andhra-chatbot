1.Which of the following is most directly linked to improving primary sector GVA?

A. Setting up agro-processing units for local produce

B. Increasing crop productivity, livestock output and irrigation efficiency

C. Developing rural tourism and hospitality facilities

D. Expanding market yards and financial service access

2. Value addition improves local GVA mainly because it:

A. Increases the economic value of raw produce through processing or branding

B. Increases cultivated area without changing production practices

C. Reduces the need for market linkage by focusing only on production

D. Shifts all agricultural workers into non-farm employment

3. Which intervention is most appropriate for improving secondary sector GVA?

A. Establishing food processing, packaging and manufacturing units

B. Promoting crop diversification and micro-irrigation

C. Developing tourism circuits and local hospitality services

D. Expanding access to banking and digital payments

4. Which of the following best represents a tertiary sector activity?

A. Milk production and poultry rearing

B. Mango cultivation and oil palm farming

C. Tourism, logistics, trade and financial services

D. Rice milling and cashew processing

5. The main purpose of analyzing sector-wise GVA contribution is to:

A. Identify key growth drivers and prioritize sector-specific interventions

B. Calculate only the total number of workers in each sector

C. Estimate only scheme-wise expenditure across departments

D. Compare population growth across mandals or blocks

6. Which is the most suitable outcome indicator for agriculture-led GVA improvement?

A. Number of farmer meetings conducted during the year

B. Number of agriculture-related circulars issued by departments

C. Increase in area under high-value crops and micro-irrigation

D. Number of officials trained on agriculture schemes

7. Post-harvest infrastructure contributes to higher GVA by:

A. Improving storage, grading, processing and price realization

B. Increasing the number of farmers registered under schemes

C. Expanding irrigation coverage for traditional crops

D. Reducing the need for private sector participation

8. Departmental convergence is important for improving local economic growth because:

A. GVA growth requires coordinated action across production, infrastructure, skills, finance and markets

B. Each department can independently achieve GVA growth without coordination

C. It mainly helps in preparing more detailed administrative reports

D. It ensures that only one department is responsible for all economic outcomes

9. A strong review and accountability system for GVA improvement should focus on:

A. Tracking meetings, expenditure and number of instructions issued

B. Tracking baseline, targets, progress, bottlenecks and corrective action

C. Tracking only physical assets created under different schemes

D. Tracking beneficiary coverage without linking it to economic outcomes
