EVOLUTION OF THE INDUSTRY

Pre-1980s

Traditional Craft Origins

Clay roofing tiles (nalia), wall clocks, and local earthenware. A locally-rooted, labour-intensive, informal industry.

1980s–90s

First Major Shift

Transition from traditional products to modern ceramic tiles. Mosaic and galicha tiles replaced clay roofing as primary output.

Mid-1990s

The Game Changer

Collective adoption of automation, digital printing & spool-roller tunnel firing — the defining technological turning point.

2000s–Now

Global Standing

High-end vitrified tiles, sanitaryware, and large-format slabs. India becomes the world's \#2 ceramic tile producer.

THE SUPPORT ECOSYSTEM

INDUSTRY INITIATIVES

Morbi Ceramic Association (MCA)

Umbrella body enabling cooperation — fraud prevention, order-sharing via subcontracting, and unified representation to state authorities.

Community Cohesion

Patel-Prajapati networks foster mutual support during financial stress, enabling informal credit and minimising conflict.

Collective Lobbying

Unified voice successfully secured GST cuts, anti-dumping duties, and infrastructure investments.

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT

Critical Infrastructure

Dedicated gas pipelines, reliable power supply, road network linking to Kandla & Mundra ports for seamless export logistics.

Anti-Dumping Duties

Central government tariffs shield domestic manufacturers from low-cost Chinese imports and price undercutting.

GST Rate Reduction

Tax rate cut from 28% to 18% after industry protests, significantly boosting domestic consumption.

4

CHALLENGES FACING THE CLUSTER

Global Competition

Chinese manufacturers with massive scale & lower costs pressure both domestic and export markets persistently.

Environmental Regulation

NGT ordered closure of coal gasifiers, forcing costly transition to LNG to reduce SO₂ and particulate pollution.

Labour Informality

Reliance on migrant & casual workers — dust hazards, no social security, no permanent employment status.

Urban Sprawl & Finance

Cash crunches + ribbon development, water scarcity, and unplanned land growth along highways.
