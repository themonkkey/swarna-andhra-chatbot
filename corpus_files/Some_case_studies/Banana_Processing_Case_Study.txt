OFFICER TAKEAWAY

Banana is not only a fruit crop. It is a full value chain: fruit, flower, pseudo-stem, fibre, sap, scutcher and central core can each be turned into income-generating products.

Fresh fruit glut can be managed through chips, flour, powder, candy, halwa and pickles.

Pseudo-stem disposal cost can be converted into farmer income and rural jobs.

District officers can converge horticulture, DRDA/SHG, PMFME, MSME and FPO support.

BANANA PROCESSING CASE STUDIES

Case Study 2: Jalgaon — pseudo-stem waste-to-wealth cluster

THE PROBLEM

Pseudo-stem after harvest was commonly thrown on field boundaries and burnt after drying.

Farmers faced disposal cost without income from a bulky biomass resource.

Jalgaon district alone generated around 38.5 lakh MT pseudo-stem biomass annually.

THE OPPORTUNITY

Extract fibre, sap and scutcher close to farms.

Use sap for organic liquid fertilizer and scutcher for compost/vermi-compost.

Create village-level cottage units operated by local farmer SHGs.

70–80 MT/ha

Pseudo-stem availability after harvest

55,000–60,000 ha

BANANA PROCESSING CASE STUDIES

Lessons for Andhra Pradesh district and mandal officials

Start with clusters

Select banana-dense villages so raw material aggregation is viable.

Keep first processing local

Bulky pseudo-stem cannot travel long distances economically.

Use SHGs/FPOs

Women and farmer groups are ideal operators for food products and cottage units.

Standardise products

Recipes, packaging, label, FSSAI, batch tracking and quality control are essential.

Converge schemes

Horticulture + DRDA/SHG + PMFME + MSME + bank credit + KVK support.

Track outcomes

Measure income, jobs, waste processed, product sales and repeat buyers.

Do not begin with machinery purchase alone. Begin with cluster selection, entrepreneurs, technical recipes, market testing and confirmed raw material flow.

Sources: TNAU Agritech Portal success story; RKVY/Tapti Valley Banana Pseudo-stem case document

BANANA PROCESSING CASE STUDIES

How AP districts can adapt the models

Fresh banana processing

Chips, flour, fruit powder, halwa, candy, pickle and ready-to-eat snacks.

Best for SHG/FPO-led units near production and consumption centres.

Use local fairs, schools/hostels, tourism points and retail shops for market testing.

Pseudo-stem processing

Fibre extraction, sap collection, scutcher composting and central value addition.

Best for banana clusters with adequate post-harvest pseudo-stem availability.

Small units reduce transport cost; central unit handles branding and bulk sale.

District-level enablers

District product plan and value-chain map.

Tie-up with KVK/TNAU/NRCB/food technology institutes.

Common brand, FSSAI handholding, packaging and buyer linkage.

Monthly review through agriculture/horticulture and livelihood KPIs.

Ideal first step: identify one banana cluster per district and run a 100-day pilot before scaling.

Sources: TNAU Agritech Portal success story; RKVY/Tapti Valley Banana Pseudo-stem case document

Closing message

For district and mandal officials, the banana case studies show how a crop-specific value chain can move from “production monitoring” to “income, enterprise and employment generation”.

Action after training: select one banana cluster, one food product pilot and one pseudo-stem waste-to-wealth pilot.
