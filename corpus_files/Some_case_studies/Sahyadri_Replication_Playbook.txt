The Problem We Are Trying to Solve

Climate stress

Erratic weather, unpredictable water, and pests put fragile smallholder incomes at constant risk.

Fragmented supply chains

Poor coordination among players raises cost and blocks access to markets, quality inputs and timely information.

Post-harvest losses

Around 40% of produce never reaches the market due to weak storage, processing and logistics.

Tiny, scattered holdings

Small & marginal farmers cannot reach economies of scale alone; past co-operatives largely under-delivered.

The Answer: Aggregate Farmers into an FPC

A Farmer Producer Company (FPC) is registered under the Companies Act, owned and democratically run by farmer-members, with professional managers handling day-to-day operations.

It lets small farmers act at scale — managing the whole chain from input to market.

Five Pillars Behind the Results

Agro-advisory teams

Experts guide crop choice, cultivation, pest & soil management — building farmer skills.

Pack-house & processing

Sorting, grading, packaging plus dehydration/pulping/canning add value & consistency.

Cold storage & logistics

Optimal temperature/humidity extends shelf life and slashes post-harvest loss.

Modern tech

Precision agriculture, GPS, drones and drip irrigation cut input waste, raise yields.

Blockchain & ERP

Full traceability and transparent, fair-trade pricing — a first among Indian FPCs.

Frame these as the investment shopping-list. Pillars 1 and 5 (advisory + traceability) are relatively low-capital and can start early; pillars 2-4 (pack-house, cold chain, precision tech) are heavier infrastructure built over time as turnover grows.

Both sides win — that mutual benefit is what makes the model politically and commercially durable. For officers: farmer income gains build trust in the FPC, which drives membership, which builds scale. It is a virtuous loop.

Replication Roadmap for Your Mandal

1

Identify & mobilise

Map crops with surplus/loss; convene smallholders around one or two high-value crops. Start small — Sahyadri began with 4 farmers.

2

Form / revive the FPC

Register or re-activate an FPC. Lock in farmer ownership + professional management from day one.

3

Build shared infrastructure

Phase pack-house, grading and cold storage as volumes grow. Pool member capital + leverage govt & FI partnerships.

4

Adopt technology & traceability

Introduce agro-advisory, precision inputs, drip irrigation, and ERP/traceability for transparent pricing.

5

Link to markets & scale

Secure direct buyers, retail, e-commerce and exports. Reinvest surplus; expand crops and villages.

Challenges to Plan For

Climate & water risk

Drought-resistant varieties, precision/drip irrigation, climate-smart practices.

Soil & resource overuse

Organic fertilisers, crop rotation, integrated pest management to protect long-term fertility.

Heavy capital needs

Phase infrastructure investment; blend member capital with FI and government partnerships.

Coordination across stakeholders

Engage farmers, govt, research bodies & private partners; share knowledge and risk.

Staying competitive

Embed continuous improvement — feedback, evaluation and innovation at every level.

What Officers Should Take Away

Aggregation is the unlock

An FPC turns scattered small farmers into a market force. Without scale, none of the rest works.

Own the chain, not just the farm

Value leaks at every handoff. Capturing processing, storage and sales keeps margins with farmers.

Sequence the investment

Start with people & advisory; build pack-house, cold chain and tech as revenue grows.

Technology builds trust

ERP, blockchain and traceability convert quality into premium prices and loyal buyers.

Start small, think long

From 4 farmers to ₹1,000 Cr — patient, compounding growth, not a one-off scheme.
