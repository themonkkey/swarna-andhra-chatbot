THE CONCEPT

What is Biofloc Fish Farming?

Biofloc is a high-density fish farming method carried out in a controlled tank, where naturally occurring microbes convert fish waste into useful protein-rich nutrients — recycled back as feed.

Controlled environment

Farming happens in a single tank, not an open pond — usable even where no water body exists.

Waste becomes feed

Microbial 'floc' converts fish waste into nutrients, cutting feed cost and pollution.

High density, small space

Large yields from a compact footprint set up beside the household.

WHY IT SUITS RURAL HOUSEHOLDS

No pond or river required

Set up next to the home

Low land requirement

Family-run, low labour

Steady 8-month income cycle

Reuses water & feed efficiently

ACTION PLAN

Replication Roadmap for Your Mandal

PHASE 1

Survey & Select

Map SC/landless households lacking water-body access

Identify motivated families willing to form an SHG

Confirm space (\~40 m²) and power/water near homes

PHASE 2

Mobilise & Fund

Form & register the self-help group

Tap SCSP / state fishery & NRLM convergence funds

Link group to a bank account for the cycle

PHASE 3

Set Up & Train

Install biofloc tank; supply fry, feed & test kit

Hands-on training on water quality & feeding

Source GIFT fry from a certified hatchery

PHASE 4

Run & Scale

Mentor through the first 8-month cycle

Track growth, costs & sales as a demo unit

Use success to onboard more groups

FOR THE OFFICER

Key Factors for a Successful Replication

Convergence

Combine SCSP, fishery dept & NRLM schemes for capital and subsidy.

Technical hand-holding

Tie up with a research/KVK partner for continuous guidance like CMFRI.

Quality inputs

Use certified GIFT fry and reliable feed — the single biggest yield driver.

Water discipline

Daily water-quality monitoring decides survival and growth rates.

Group ownership

A genuine SHG with shared stake sustains the unit beyond officials.

Demo-led scaling

Run one model unit well, then let visible income recruit the next groups.

Pick one ward. Form one group. Build one tank. Prove the income — then replicate.
