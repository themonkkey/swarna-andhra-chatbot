The Case for Ethanol — Why Now

Import dependence

India imports over 85% of its crude oil; every litre of blended ethanol displaces imported fuel and saves foreign exchange.

Untapped feedstock surplus

Nellore's paddy, maize and sugarcane belts generate surplus and damaged grain that fetch limited value beyond the food-grain market.

Volatile farm incomes

Single-crop dependence and price swings leave paddy and cane growers exposed to weak, unpredictable seasonal returns.

Rising national demand

The push toward 20% blending (E20) is opening assured, long-term offtake for new distillery capacity across India.

The Answer: National Ethanol Blending Programme

Under the EBP, Oil Marketing Companies purchase ethanol from molasses- and grain-based distilleries at pre-fixed prices for blending into petrol. It lets districts like Nellore convert surplus grain and cane into a guaranteed second market.

Five Pillars for Building Nellore's Ethanol Capacity

Feedstock aggregation & FPCs

Organise paddy, maize and cane growers so distilleries get consistent, quality-graded volumes.

Distillery capacity

Attract new dual-feed (grain + molasses) distilleries, or expand existing sugar-mill capacity.

Assured OMC offtake

Long-term supply agreements with Oil Marketing Companies de-risk investment and lock in prices.

Port & logistics

Krishnapatnam Port and NH16 connectivity support feedstock movement and by-product export.

By-product utilisation

DDGS as cattle feed and bio-CNG from spent wash add income and cut waste.

Development Roadmap for Nellore

1

Assess feedstock surplus

Map paddy, maize and molasses surplus by mandal; identify volumes viable for a distillery-ready supply chain.

2

Attract distillery investment

Court dual-feed (grain + molasses) distillery investment, or support expansion of existing sugar mills.

3

Secure OMC offtake agreements

Tie up long-term ethanol supply contracts with Oil Marketing Companies before capacity is built.

4

Build farmer aggregation

Strengthen FPCs/FPOs so growers can supply consistent, graded feedstock volumes.

5

Scale via by-products & logistics

Add DDGS and bio-CNG lines; route exports and inputs through Krishnapatnam Port.

Challenges to Plan For

Feedstock price volatility

Grain and cane prices can swing with the food market, affecting distillery economics.

Water stress

Cane cultivation is water-intensive; distilleries also need managed effluent and water use.

Capital intensity

New distillery and storage infrastructure requires significant upfront investment.

Food-versus-fuel concerns

Diverting grain to ethanol must be balanced against local food and fodder needs.

Logistics bottlenecks

Moving feedstock and by-products smoothly needs coordinated road, rail and port capacity.

Take Away

Ethanol is a new demand stream

It turns surplus and damaged grain into an assured, price-linked market.

Offtake de-risks investment

OMC agreements guarantee buyers before a rupee of distillery capital is spent.

By-products add a second revenue layer

DDGS and bio-CNG turn waste streams into additional income.

Infrastructure is a natural advantage

Krishnapatnam Port and NH16 give Nellore logistics few districts can match.

Start with pilots, scale with policy

Anchor around one distillery and one crop belt, then expand as volumes and offtake grow.
