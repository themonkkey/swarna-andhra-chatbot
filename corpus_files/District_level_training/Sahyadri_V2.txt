CASE STUDY • FARMER PRODUCER ORGANISATIONS

The Problem Every Smallholder Farmer Faces

Fragmented Holdings

Small & marginal farmers cannot reach economies of scale alone. A 1-acre farmer cannot fulfill a 500-tonne buyer order — she sells to intermediaries at the worst price.

Fragmented Supply Chains

Poor coordination raises costs and blocks market access. Traders and retailers capture 70–80% of consumer price — the farmer is a price-taker in someone else's infrastructure.

Post-Harvest Loss \~25–35%

No cold storage, no pack-house, forced sales at harvest when all farmers sell simultaneously and prices collapse. Loss is structural — not a failure of effort.

Locked Out of Credit & Inputs

Formal banks require collateral. Without it, farmers borrow from input dealers at 24–36% interest — who then dictate which inputs to buy. Income and agency are both lost.

Climate Stress

Erratic weather, unpredictable water, and pests put fragile smallholder incomes at constant risk — with no institutional buffer to absorb the shocks.

CASE STUDY • FARMER PRODUCER ORGANISATIONS

The Answer: Aggregate Farmers into an FPC

A Farmer Producer Company (FPC) is registered under the Companies Act — owned and democratically run by farmer-members, with professional managers handling day-to-day operations. It lets small farmers act at scale, managing the entire chain from input to market.

WHAT AN FPC DELIVERS FOR FARMERS

Pooled resources & collective bargaining — getting wholesale input prices, premium sale prices

Access to formal finance — pooled equity becomes collateral; banks lend to FPCs where they won't to individuals

Professional management with farmer ownership — the separation cooperatives failed to maintain

Market linkage to corporate buyers, retail chains, and export markets at volume

Built-in governance — each member owns shares, profits distributed as patronage bonus proportional to business done

CASE STUDY • FARMER PRODUCER ORGANISATIONS

Five Pillars Behind the Results

Agro-Advisory

Experts guide crop choice, cultivation, pest & soil management before harvest — so output matches buyer specs. This is supply-side quality control, not just advice.

Pack-house & Processing

Sorting and grading means only Grade A goes to premium buyers. Dehydration, pulping, canning open processed-food markets. Each step multiplies price per unit.

Cold Storage & Logistics

Optimal temperature/humidity extends shelf life — enabling export and allowing the FPC to sell when prices are high, not when harvest forces a sale.

Precision Technology

Drones, GPS, drip irrigation, and precision agriculture cut input waste and raise yield consistency — which corporate buyers need before committing to advance contracts.

Blockchain & ERP

Traceability is non-negotiable for EU/Japan export. ERP tracks procurement, inventory, payments — transparent pricing builds the trust that keeps 26,000 farmers in one institution.

CASE STUDY • FARMER PRODUCER ORGANISATIONS

Challenges to Plan For — These Are Not Reasons to Hesitate

CLIMATE & WATER RISK

Drought-resistant varieties, precision/drip irrigation, and climate-smart practices reduce — but do not eliminate — yield vulnerability. Design for this from day one, not as an afterthought.

HEAVY CAPITAL NEEDS

Phase infrastructure investment: pack-house first (fastest payback), cold chain second, technology third. Blend member equity with NABARD credit and government scheme financing. Front-loading capital kills FPCs.

COORDINATION ACROSS STAKEHOLDERS

Farmers, government, research institutions, private buyers — all must be aligned. The district administration's role is not ceremonial; it is the critical intermediary to the financing ecosystem.

SOIL & RESOURCE OVERUSE

Organic fertilisers, crop rotation, and integrated pest management protect long-term soil fertility — directly relevant for Prakasam's chilli belts, which face known soil depletion risks from repeated mono-cropping.

STAYING COMPETITIVE

Embed continuous improvement through feedback and evaluation. Markets and buyer requirements evolve — the FPC must evolve with them or lose the premium it worked to build.

CASE STUDY • FARMER PRODUCER ORGANISATIONS

What Should You Take Away

01

Aggregation is the unlock

Without aggregation at volume, none of the rest — processing, cold chain, export — is economically viable. Start here.

02

Own the chain, not just the farm

Value leaks at every handoff. Aggregation alone gives a slightly better mandi price. Processing + storage + sales keeps the margin.

03

Sequence the investment

People and advisory first. Pack-house when volumes justify it. Cold chain and technology after revenue grows. Do not front-load capital.

04

Technology builds trust

ERP transparent pricing and traceability keep 26,000 farmers in one institution — and open the door to premium markets that require documentation.

05

Start small. Think long.

4 farmers to ₹1,482 Cr turnover took 14 years. The temptation to design a district-wide scheme from day one is how government programmes fail to build institutional depth.

CASE STUDY • FROM LEARNING TO ACTION

There Is Already an FPC in Prakasam. So Why Ask This Question?

PRERANA CHILLI FARMERS PRODUCER COMPANY LTD

Cumbum Mandal, Prakasam • Registered under Companies Act

Address:

D No 2-104/1A, C/o Bijjam Rajeswara Reddy, Cumbum — a residential address, not a business premises

Crop focus:

Chilli — the right crop, the right district, the right GI belt

Public filings:

No turnover, no AGM date, no balance sheet traceable in public MCA records

Scheme linkage:

Not listed in SFAC national FPO directory or APEDA Farmer Connect portal

IS IT DORMANT?

We don't know for certain — but the signals are worth asking about:

Registered address is a private residence — no pack-house or operational site listed

Zero paid-up capital activity visible in public filings

No NABARD credit linkage or APEDA registration found

A neighbouring FPC in Cumbum (Horticulture, Jan 2024) shows ₹20K paid-up on ₹15L authorised — same pattern

THE SAHYADRI CONTRAST:

Sahyadri hired a professional CEO before Day 1 and never conflated farmer ownership with farmer management. That single decision is the most likely explanation for why one FPC reached ₹1,482 Cr and another may not have left the starting block.

01 DIAGNOSE PRERANA

Call the district horticulture officer today. Find Bijjam Rajeswara Reddy in Cumbum. Ask: Did Prerana receive SFAC equity grant? Does it have a bank account? Are the founding farmers still willing?

02 REVIVE OR RESTART

Reviving an existing CIN is faster and cheaper than fresh incorporation. If Prerana's founders are willing and the grant was not misused, the legal shell already exists. The missing piece is a professional CEO and an anchor buyer.

03 NAME YOUR PILOT MANDAL

Identify one mandal in Prakasam — Cumbum or otherwise — with the highest Teja/Sannam acreage and the clearest post-harvest loss. That is where Phase 1 starts. Not across the district. One mandal, one variety, twenty farmers.

CHILLI AS ANCHOR CROP • REPLICATION ROADMAP

The Prakasam Chilli FPC: A Sequenced Roadmap

Adapted from the Sahyadri model — but sequenced for chilli's different cold chain and processing logic

PHASE 1

Now

Identify & Mobilise

Map chilli mandals by post-harvest loss & Teja/Sannam area. Convene 20–40 farmers in one mandal around one variety. Do not start with multiple crops.

PHASE 2

3–6 months

Form or Revive FPC

Register FPC with SFAC equity grant support. Lock in farmer ownership + professional CEO from day one. Register with APEDA for eventual export clearance.

PHASE 3

6–18 months

Build Minimum Viable Chain

Invest in: (1) mechanical dryer — eliminates aflatoxin risk & colour loss; (2) destalking unit; (3) grading tables. Estimated ₹20–40L. Pack-house next when volumes justify.

PHASE 4

18–36 months

Process & Direct-Market

Add chilli powder unit (₹15–25L). Approach Guntur export houses directly — cutting out sub-agents. Target UAE and domestic branded buyers before attempting EU certification.

PHASE 5

3–5 years

Scale & Certify

GlobalGAP / FSSAI certification for EU/US markets. Oleoresin extraction unit. ERP + traceability. Explore FPC-to-FPC federation with other chilli mandals in Prakasam.

CRITICAL DIFFERENCE FROM SAHYADRI: Dried chilli does not require cold chain in early phases — mechanical drying and grading are the priority investments. This lowers Phase 3 capital requirements significantly versus a fresh-produce FPC.
