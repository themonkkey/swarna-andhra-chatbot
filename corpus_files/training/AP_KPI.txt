HOW TO READ

Anatomy of a KPI — What Every Row Tells You

Never look at a raw number alone. A KPI only means something against its target, its benchmark and its past.

Inland Fish Production — Eluru District — Q2 FY 2025-26

TARGET

60,000 t

ACHIEVEMENT

52,400 t

% OF TARGET

87%

BEST DISTRICT

68,200 t

TREND VS Q1

▼ down 4%

TARGET

What you were supposed to achieve this period. Set by HOD / Secretariat. Your yardstick.

ACHIEVEMENT

The actual value you entered. On its own it means nothing — always compare it.

% OF TARGET

Achievement ÷ Target. Under 80% = act now. This is your first red / amber / green signal.

BENCHMARK

The best district or best state. Shows how much better is realistically possible.

TREND

This period vs last. A number below target but rising is very different from one falling.

Illustrative KPI card. Values shown for training only.

10

HOW TO READ

The Four Questions to Ask Every KPI

Run every indicator through these four questions, in order. They turn a number into a decision.

1

Am I meeting target?

Compare achievement to target. Below 80% is a warning; below 60% is urgent.

2

Which way is it moving?

Look at the last 3 periods. Rising, flat or falling matters more than one reading.

3

How do I compare?

Rank against other districts and the best state. A big gap = room to improve.

4

So what do I do?

Decide: hold course, dig into the cause, or escalate. No KPI review ends without an action.

A simple discipline for every monthly / quarterly KPI review.

SKILL 1 — JUDGE THE GAP

Target vs Achievement: The Traffic-Light Rule

Convert every % of target into a colour. It tells you — at a glance — where to spend your attention this month.

GREEN

≥ 90% of target

On track. Keep doing what works; note it as a good practice others can copy.

Example: Immunisation 101% of target

AMBER

80 – 89% of target

Slipping. Watch closely, find the reason early, correct before it turns red.

Example: Piped water coverage at 84%

RED

Below 80% of target

Off track. Diagnose the cause now and act or escalate this week — do not wait for year-end.

Example: Secondary dropout above target

Thresholds are a practical rule of thumb; adjust to departmental norms where targets differ.

From Reading to Action — A Decision Path

Every KPI review must end in one of three decisions. This is how a district officer moves from number to action.

SUSTAIN

OFF TRACK — RED

PUT IT TO WORK

Running a Monthly KPI Review

The single highest-value habit: a short, disciplined monthly review of your district's KPIs with the departments that own them.

BEFORE

Pull the district dashboard. Sort KPIs by status — reds and ambers to the top. Note the monthly ones first.

IN THE ROOM

Spend time only on red/amber KPIs. For each: owner explains cause, agrees one action and a date. Greens get a nod.

DECIDE

Every off-track KPI leaves with an owner, an action and a deadline. Nothing is 'noted for information'.

AFTER

Record decisions. Check last month's actions were done. Escalate anything stuck to the Collector / HOD.

A practical operating rhythm for district-level KPI governance.

18

AVOID THESE

Common Mistakes in Reading KPIs

These errors quietly mislead decisions. Watch for them in your own reviews.

Reading the number alone

A value without its target, trend and benchmark tells you nothing. Always compare.

Chasing greens, ignoring reds

Time goes to what's easy to report. Spend it on the red and amber KPIs instead.

Trusting one data point

A single low month may be a data-entry gap, not a real fall. Verify before you act.

Waiting for year-end

Annual review is too late for monthly KPIs. Course-correct within the month.

Confusing activity with outcome

Money spent or meetings held ≠ result. Track the outcome KPI, not just the effort.

No action recorded

A review that ends without an owner and a date changes nothing on the ground.

Practical guidance for district & mandal officers.

198

KPIs YOU ENTER

225 KPIs to monitor & submit

YOUR CHECKLIST

You are the source of ground truth — crops, employment, health attendance, works, infrastructure.

Enter every KPI for the period, even a 'zero' or 'nil' — a blank breaks the district roll-up.

Double-check the figure and unit before you SAVE, then FORWARD to your district officer.

Once forwarded it is FROZEN — you cannot correct it, so verify first.

Prioritise the 53 monthly KPIs; they have the tightest, most frequent deadlines.
