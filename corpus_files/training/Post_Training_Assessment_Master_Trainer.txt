Objective Question

1. Which statement best explains Gross Value Added (GVA)?

A. Total expenditure by government in a district

B. Value of output after deducting intermediate consumption

C. Total tax collected from all sectors

D. Total exports minus imports

2. Why is the production approach important for district-level economic analysis?

A. It uses only population data

B. It directly links sector activity, output and input use with value added

C. It avoids the need for sector-wise data

D. It is used only for national income estimation

3. What is the main difference between GSDP and GDDP?

A. GSDP is for districts and GDDP is for the state

B. GSDP is state-level income, while GDDP is district-level income

C. GSDP includes only agriculture, while GDDP includes all sectors

D. GDDP is estimated quarterly, while GSDP is estimated monthly

4. Which of the following is a key caution while interpreting district estimates?

A. District estimates are always based on complete direct data

B. District estimates should be read with methodology, data source and validation trail

C. District estimates do not require revision

D. Proxy indicators are never used in district estimation

5. If output increases but input costs rise faster, what may happen to GVA?

A. GVA will always increase

B. GVA may decline or grow slowly

C. GVA becomes equal to taxes

D. GVA is not affected by input costs

6. When direct district-level data is unavailable, how is district sector GVA generally estimated?

A. By dividing state GVA equally among all districts

B. By using suitable district-level allocation keys or proxy indicators

C. By ignoring that sector in district estimates

D. By using only national population share

7. Which of the following is an example of a primary sector activity?

A. Banking

B. Construction

C. Fishing

D. Public administration

8. In Mandal Domestic Product estimation, what does a mandal's share of an indicator determine?

A. Its tax rate

B. Its share of allocated district GVA for that activity

C. Its population growth rate

D. Its administrative ranking only

9. Why do proxy indicators need validation?

A. Because they are always wrong

B. Because they may only indirectly represent actual economic activity

C. Because they replace all official statistics

D. Because they are used only for agriculture

10. What is the main administrative message of the presentation?

A. Data should stop at reporting

B. District officials should use economic data for diagnosis, prioritisation and follow-up action

C. GDDP estimates are not useful for planning

D. Only state-level officers can influence economic management
