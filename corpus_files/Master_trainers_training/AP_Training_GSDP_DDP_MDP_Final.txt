The same economic activity can be understood by sector, by state, and by district — but the data availability and estimation difficulty increase as we go below the state level.

India's Data Hierarchy

GDP

National level. Estimated by MoSPI. Released every quarter.

GSDP

State level. Released annually by the State DES (e.g., DES for AP).

DDP — District Domestic Product

District level. Annual. Published by the Directorate of Economics and Statistics.

The Building Block: Gross State Value Added (GSVA)

GSVA = Output − Intermediate Consumption

GSDP = Σ GSVA across all sectors in the district (at Basic Prices)

| Illustrative example | Amount |
| :-: | :-: |
| Value of rice produced (Output) | ₹100 |
| Seeds, fertiliser, electricity, transport and other inputs (Inputs) | ₹40 |
| Gross Value Added | ₹60 |

Important: higher output does not always mean higher GSVA if input costs rise faster or if prices fall.

The sectoral economy: primary, secondary and tertiary

Useful grouping for district diagnosis, but not sufficient by itself

| Broad sector | Typical sub-sectors | District planning relevance |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
| Primary | Agriculture (crops), livestock, forestry, fishing, mining | Production base, natural resources, rural livelihoods |
| Secondary | Manufacturing, construction, electricity, gas, water supply and other utilities | Industrial activity, infrastructure, urban expansion |
| Tertiary | Trade, transport, hotels, finance, real estate, public services, etc. | Market activity, logistics, service delivery, urban economy |

District action requires sub-sector specificity: "services are growing" is less useful than "logistics and tourism indicators are improving while formal trade is stagnant."

• At current prices, estimates generally use current year production or quantities multiplied by current year prices.

• At constant (2011-12) prices, estimates generally use current year production or quantities multiplied by base year prices, or deflation using relevant CPI/WPI/indices.

• For activities where direct state-level data are not available, national estimates are allocated using suitable state-wise indicators.

• For unincorporated or household activities, benchmark survey estimates are moved using relevant growth indicators.

Value of output = current year production * current year price

Introduction to the Tertiary Sector

The tertiary sector covers market and non-market services that support households, enterprises and government operations.

Market services

Trade, hotels, transport, communication, financial services, real estate, professional services and information services.

Government services

Public administration, defence and public delivery of education, health and other services.

Household services

Unincorporated enterprises, own-account workers, domestic services and ownership of dwellings.

GSDP at market prices = GSVA at basic prices + Product taxes – Product subsidies

For sectoral analysis, the core measurement unit is GSVA. GSDP is obtained after adding net product taxes.

Why district estimates are harder than state estimates

More local detail usually means more data constraints

1

Data availability

Some sector data exists only at state level or with weak district identifiers.

2

Informality

A large share of activity may be unregistered or not reported in routine systems.

3

Headquarter effects

Services or company records may be booked in one location but delivered elsewhere.

4

Proxy dependence

District estimates often require allocation keys or proxy indicators.

5

Comparability risk

Districts differ in area, population, sector mix and administrative capacity.

Therefore, district estimates should always be read with their methodology, data source and validation trail.

State estimates and district apportionment

When direct district GVA is unavailable, allocation keys become important

State sector GVA

District allocation key

District-wise sector GVA

• A mixed approach is used.

• GDDP = GDVA + Taxes on Products – Subsidies on Products

• NDDP = NDVA + Taxes on Products – Subsidies on Products

• Per Capita Income =GDDP/ projected population for the year for the district

GDDP Estimation Methodology

District Domestic Product estimates support decentralised planning by comparing inter-district economic structure, performance and sectoral disparities.

State sector estimate

Official GVA / GSDP estimate at state level

District data / allocator

Direct district production data where available; proxy or physical indicator where direct data is weak

District sector GVA

Sector-wise value allocated or estimated for each district

GDDP / PCI

Aggregate district economy and per capita income for planning

Core rule

Use the same sectoral logic as state accounts wherever district-level data are available. Where data are scarce, apportion state estimates using the best available district indicator.

Key caution

For many non-primary sectors, district estimates are broad indicators because direct district data remain limited and estimates are revised when firmer data arrive.

Primary sector methodology I: Agriculture, horticulture and livestock

Agriculture + Horticulture

• Major crops: district-wise value of output = district production × district price.

• If district prices are unavailable, use regional / neighbouring district prices where available.

• If no regional price is available, use state-level prices.

• Miscellaneous/unspecified crops: if only district area is available, value of output = district area × state-level VPH of similar crops.

• Inputs are allocated across districts in proportion to district GVO before deriving district GVA.

Livestock

• Milk, meat, wool, cocoons and honey: district production × average wholesale prices.

• Meat by-products: state GVO allocated by district-wise meat production.

• Dung: state GVO allocated by district-wise bovine, sheep and goat population.

• Eggs: state GVO allocated by district-wise egg production.

• Poultry meat: state GVO allocated by district-wise poultry population.

• Increment in livestock: state GVO allocated by district-wise livestock population.

Primary sector methodology II: Forestry, fishing and mining

Forestry & Logging

• Major and minor forest produce: state GVA apportioned by district-wise forest area.

• Trees Outside Forest (TOF): state GVA apportioned by area under pulp and timber.

• Fuel wood: state GVA apportioned by rural and urban population.

• Fodder: state GVA apportioned by livestock projected population based on 2019 Census.

Fishing

• District-wise value of inland fish, marine fish and prawns is estimated by multiplying district-wise production with corresponding prices.

• This is closer to direct estimation than proxy apportionment when district production and prices are available.

Mining & Quarrying

• State GVA is allocated to districts on the basis of district-wise production of major and minor minerals.

• District production data should be reconciled with mining records and mineral-wise reporting.

A proxy should be used only when it is the best available indicator and the limitation is disclosed in the methodology note.

Interpretation

The mandal with a higher share of paddy production receives a higher share of the district paddy GVA. This does not imply higher productivity unless yield, prices, and input costs are separately examined.

Wrap-up: the five takeaways

1

GVA measures value added

Not just production, sales or expenditure.

2

GSDP/GDDP need methodology

Always read estimates with source, method and revision status.

3

Validation is central

Triangulate official estimates, administrative data and proxies.

4

Action closes the loop

Measurement becomes management only through assigned follow-up.

Final message: data should not end at reporting; it should improve diagnosis, prioritisation and administrative decisions.
